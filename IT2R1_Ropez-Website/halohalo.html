Preamble

The word “haluhalo” or Halo-Halo can translate to something that’s been mixed together like this halo-halo dessert.

The Halo-Halo Recipe is truly a Pinoy dessert which contains shaved ice, evaporated milk, sugar or some syrup, sweet beans, sweet banana, nata de coco, garbanzos, jelly, macapuno, sago, gulaman, sweet jackfruit, ube halaya and leche flan although it doesn’t necessarily mean you need to have all ingredients to make it but the shaved ice, evaporated milk and sugar needs to be there all the time. This is the best way to do it if you want to know how to make Halo-Halo.

Preparation (good for 4 servings)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of shaved ice
  • 1 ripe large banana
  • 1 cup of young shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup of sweet corn or chickpeas (garbanzos)
  • 2 cups of evaporated milk
  • 1 cup of firm gelatin set into a gel and cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 2 ripe mangoes sliced into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 cup of ripe jackfruit
  • 1 cup of cooked sweet yams or ube halaya
  • 4 scoops of favorite ice cream
  • 1/2 cup of rice pop

Materials

  • 4 tall cups or 4 bowls
  • 4 spoons

Steps

  1. Divide each ingredient into 4 equal parts.
  2. Get each glass, and place each solid ingredient by layer
  3. Pour one half cup of shaved icea into each glass
  4. Pour a quarter of evaporated milk over the ice in each glass
  5. Put a scoop of ice cream on top
  6. Drizzle some nuts or rice crispies on top of the ice cream

Final Words

Many people who aren’t familiar with this sweet concoction would think that halo-halo is only good during the summer but it’s just as good during colder days. With a dessert this sweet and tasty, you’d definitely want to come back for seconds and thirds.

But between buying pre-made and cooking yourself, making your own halo-halo from scratch is always better than buying from restaurants. Not only are you able to make many servings as you can, you also save money by preparing it at home. If you want to know how to make Halo-Halo, just follow this recipe.
